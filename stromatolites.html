stromatolites.

Stromatolites are layered, accretionary structures formed in the shallow water by trapping, binding and cementation of sedimentary grains by microbial mats of microorganisms, especially cyanobacteria. Fossilized stromatolites provide ancient records of life on Earth. They exhibit a variety of forms and structures. Although stromatolites are usually associated with biological activity, some possess features that are more consistent with abiotic precipitation. Peak of their diversity was reached about 1.25 billion years ago and subsequently declined in abundance and diversity later. Modern stromatolites are rare and are mostly found in hypersaline lakes or mari ne lagoons where extreme conditions due to high saline levels prevent animal grazing.
